Digital Library Development Center (DLDC)

The Digital Library Development Center collaborates with library staff, university faculty, departments and other groups on campus, and colleagues at other institutions, to enable discovery of the library's information resources, to make the campus community aware of the library's services, to build digital collections, and to ensure the persistence and usability of the library's digital resources over time.

Core activities include designing, building, and maintaining web sites, databases, digital collections, and the Library Digital Repository; installing and supporting commercial systems that provide access to information resources; administering the network of information server computers which underlie the digital library and support related initiatives on campus; researching, evaluating, and implementing new technologies; participating in national and international digital library initiatives; and documenting and sharing procedures, policies, and information about our work.

Tools used have included the Greenstone digital library software, from the New Zealand Digital Library Project at the University of Waikato, the California Digital Library's eXtensible Text Framework (XTF), Nand: A Non-relational Database, developed in house, the MySQL relational database, the SQLite software library, and the MarkLogic XML/NoSQL/Semantic Web document- and data-storing and indexing environment. Programming languages have included PHP, Python, JavaScript, OCaml, Common LISP and Erlang. The DLDC also maintains an SFX link server, supports the Luna Imaging software environment for library and campus collections, and administers server computers running the FreeBSD and Linux operating systems.

With Whom We Work

The DLDC collaborates with other groups in the library and on campus. These include The ARTFL Project, the University of Chicago Library's Electronic Text Services(ETS), IT Services Academic Technologies, the Special Collections Research Center and the Preservation Department of the University of Chicago Library, the Oriental Institute's Online Cultural and Historical Research Environment (OCHRE), the University of Chicago's Research Computing Center and the Visual Resources Center.
